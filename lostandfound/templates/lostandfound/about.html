About FindersKeepers

FindersKeepers is a convenient way for students, faculty, and staff to reconnect with their lost items without dumping personal data onto public platforms. Instead of scrolling through endless snapchat stories, our site is all about simple, direct communication: connecting the person who finds an item with the person who owns it.

How it works

Finders = People who find an item.
Keepers = People who lost the item - the original owner!

  • Post a find: Finders create short, anonymous listings without photos or identifying details.
  • Subscribe to keywords: Keepers follow keywords like “red hoodie”, “ID card”, or “headphones” and get notified when a match appears.
  • Exchange info: If a listing seems familiar, the keeper can connect with the finder via the 'contact' button. An automatic email will be sent to the finder, in hopes of connecting the lost item back to its owner!
    • We encourage finders to verify proof of ownership before returning the item, including through pictures via email.
    • We are not responsible for incorrect exchanges.

Why we built it

Traditional lost and found boards are messy, easy to impersonate, and often exclude people who don’t use certain social platforms. Our goal is to build a trustworthy, campus-wide tool that:

  • Protects user privacy with anonymous listings and limited data collection.
  • Makes impostor claims harder by shifting proof to real owners.
  • Is accessible to the whole campus community, not just one app or social site.
