About Campus Lost & Found

Campus Lost & Found is a lightweight, privacy-respecting way for students, faculty, and staff to reconnect with lost items without dumping personal data onto public platforms. Instead of scrolling through endless photo dumps, our app focuses on a simple workflow: from the person who finds an item to the person who can prove they own it.

How it works

  • Post a find: Finders create short, anonymous listings without photos or identifying details.
  • Subscribe to keywords: Keepers (people who lost items) follow keywords like “red hoodie,” “ID card,” or “headphones” and get notified when a match appears.
  • Prove ownership: If a listing looks right, the keeper sends a private confirming photo to show they really own the item before any chatting starts.
  • Safe relay chat: Once verified, a temporary relay chat opens so the finder and keeper can arrange a handoff. Messages are filtered for spam/profanity and aren’t stored long-term.

Why we built it

Traditional lost and found boards are messy, easy to impersonate, and often exclude people who don’t use certain social platforms. Our goal is to build a trustworthy, campus-wide tool that:

  • Protects user privacy with anonymous listings and limited data collection.
  • Makes impostor claims harder by shifting proof to real owners.
  • Stays accessible to the whole campus community, not just one app or social site.

Project team & support

This app was developed as a DIDA 425 capstone project with guidance from Dr. Jacopo Mazzoni. Our team shares three core values: compromise, ambition, and punctuality. We’re committed to maintaining and improving the site so it continues to run smoothly for the campus community.
